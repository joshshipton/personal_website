More About Me

I immensely enjoy the connection between cognition and technical thinking and athletic performance. and occasionally, I sometimes share my insights on grappling through articles on Medium. I started out studying philosophy at university but after two years became frustrated with the high level of abstraction and lack of real-world application and switched over to computer science. I like to believe that studying philosophy has equipped me with valuable skills such as critical thinking and analytical reasoning that I use to be a better developer, writer, and well-rounded individual. But it probably just familiarized me with esoteric language and headaches.
